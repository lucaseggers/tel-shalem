A young brown-haired woman smirked wryly, continuing some broader criticism: "... and I'm from America, you know. Born and raised, but, I am *so* glad I left. You can't say anything there anymore, someone's always offended, always has a problem with who you are. And I was in a part of Texas where there weren't many Jewish families around. When I got old enough, I knew I'd had it, and I came here.

I'm a citizen now and that means serving in the military. I'm proud of that." A pregnant pause followed; no one knew where to take our banter after that. One of the grad students further back spoke up, saying jovially, "Elath, tell them about your service!" Other Bar-Ilan students murmured.
