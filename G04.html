The greatest concern on Highway 90 ended up being not the humans but the goats. Well, goats led by humans. Herders have roamed this landscape since before the invention of the written word, and I feel a great deal of respect for those whose lives revolve around animals even today. However, that respect did not make the Kia Sorento any stronger or more goat-resistant. In the backseat, Emily helped by pretending to be a BBC newscaster, letting me know when there were herds up ahead with her “GOAT WATCH!” network program. Some were just a handful of billies, but others were hundreds strong, stank to high heaven, and took just as long to cross the road as you’d expect.

I saw very few signs or marked crossings for the herders; they just seem to traverse the high-speed traffic wherever the goats pleased. Even on this road hemmed in by borders (both invisible and tangible), nature defies constraints.
