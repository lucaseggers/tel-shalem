Just as we wrapped up the first dig day – at around 12 pm, thanks to the summer heat – we heard the sounds of boots on gravel. Someone was coming up the path. Soon, three or four local men popped into view. They were all wearing plain T-shirts and work pants, and they all smiled and shouted friendly greetings. A couple of them had both hands at their bellies, gingerly carrying something heavy.

It turned out the land directly between the dig site and the West Bank fence was actually a watermelon patch! We had arrived just after harvest time, and the farmers wanted to share the fruit of the field with us. The men sat alongside us in the sorely-needed shade cast by our shipping container, chatting with Daniel and Beriah as we all ate lunch. I have no idea what our colleagues and the workers said to each other, but I do know that those slices were the best watermelon I’ve ever eaten in my life.
