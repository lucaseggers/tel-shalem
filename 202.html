A few of us accepted, so I drove us over, following their vans to the end of a dirt road. We soon found that 'the place' was a large irrigation channel, funneling clean water between fields and fish farms. Thick patches of tallgrasses, each maybe ten feet tall and tipped with a delicate yellow seed tuft, hid this manmade oasis from the harsh heat of the outside world.

Clad in nothing but my swim trunks, I hesitated at first to jump from the slippery rock face into the channel, but soon I plugged my nose and took the plunge. The water was cold - all the better to beat the heat! Jack, Claire, Raina and I eyed each other, laughing while shivering.
