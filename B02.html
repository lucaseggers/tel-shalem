Whenever I looked up from the floors I was uncovering and saw the northwest horizon, covered in hills and dusty steppes, I couldn’t help but feel like we were somewhere remote, isolated from the world.

That notion was, of course, completely incorrect. I was working in one of the most contested regions on Earth. If I wanted to, I could’ve thrown a handful of dirt and hit the barbed wire fence mere meters to my south that encloses the West Bank. So I really should not have been surprised when we started having visitors.
